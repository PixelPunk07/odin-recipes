Adobong Manok

Description:

Adobong manok is a classic Filipino dish made by braising chicken in a savory blend of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Known for its rich, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor, it's one of the most beloved comfort foods in the Philippines and a staple in many Filipino households.

Ingredients:

  • 1kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • 1 medium onion, chopped (optional but commonly used)
  • 6-8 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1-2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional for a slightly sweet finish)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Marinate the chicken in soy sauce, garlic, and peppercorns for at least 30 minutes (optional but adds flavor).
  2. Heat oil in a pan and saut√© the garlic (if not included in the marinade) and onions until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken pieces (with the marinade) and cook until lightly browned.
  4. Pour in the vinegar and avoid stirring; let it boil for 2-3 minutes to cook off the sharp acidity.
  5. Add water and bay leaves, then bring to a boil.
  6. Add brown sugar if using, then adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or more soy sauce to taste.
  7. Simmer uncovered for a few more minutes to reduce the sauce to your preferred thickness.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.
