Sinigang na Baboy

Description:

Sinigang na baboy is a beloved Filipino soup known for its comforting sour broth made with tamarind. Packed with tender pork, fresh vegetables, and a vibrant tangy flavor, it's a hearty dish that perfectly balances savory and sour â€” a true staple in many Filipino homes.

Main Ingredients:

  • 1-1.5lbs (500-700g) pork belly, ribs, or shoulder
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2-3 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 6-8 cups water
  • 1 packet tamarind soup mix (or fresh tamarind if preferred)

Vegetables:

  • 1 radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1-2 eggplants, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 1 cup sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2-3 okra (optional)
  • 1 green chili (siling pansigang)

Seasoning:

  • Fish Sauce (patis), to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps:

  1. In a pot, place the pork and add enough water to cover. Bring to a boil, then skim off the scum.
  2. Add the onions and tomatoes. Lower the heat and let it simmer until the pork becomes tender (about 45-60 minutes).
  3. Put in the radish and cook for 3-5 minutes. Then add eggplant, sitaw, and okra (if using). Continue simmering until slightly tender.
  4. Pour in the tamarind mix (or fresh tamarind juice). Stir and adjust sourness to taste.
  5. Add fish sauce, salt, or pepper as needed.
  6. Put the kangkong and green chili. Simmer for another 1-2 minutes, then turn off the heat.
  7. Enjoy with steamed rice!
