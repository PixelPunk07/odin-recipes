Tinolang Manok

Description:

Tinolang Manok is a classic Filipino chicken soup known for its light and savory broth, infused with ginger, garlic, and onion. Traditionally, it features tender chicken pieces, green papaya or chayote, and flavorful leafy greens like chili leaves or malunggay (moringa). This comforting dish is often enjoyed as a hearty meal, especially during rainy days or when someone is feeling under the weather.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1-1.5kg), cut into serving pieces
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced into strips
  • 1-2 green chili peppers (optional)
  • 4 cups of water (or chicken stock for richer flavor)
  • 1 small green papaya (or sayote), peeled and sliced
  • 1 bunch of chili leaves or malunggay (moringa) leaves
  • Fish sauce or salt, to taste
  • Ground black pepper, to taste

Steps:

  1. Clean and cut the chicken into serving pieces.
  2. In a pot, heat cooking oil over medium heat. Saut√© ginger, garlic, and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken pieces and cook for 3-5 minutes, lightly browning them.
  4. Pour in water (or chicken stock) and bring to a boil. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface.
  5. Add fish sauce or salt and pepper to taste.
  6. When the chicken is nearly cooked, add green papaya or chayote slices and simmer until tender.
  7. Stir in chili leaves or malunggay leaves. Cook for 1-2 minutes until wilted.
  8. Taste the broth and add more fish sauce or pepper if needed.
  9. Ladle into bowls and enjoy with steamed rice.
